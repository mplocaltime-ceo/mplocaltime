# Data Retention Schedule

This retention schedule is provided for counsel review and helps document how Mpumalanga Local Time retains personal information in a manner aligned with POPIA, GDPR and applicable legal requirements.

## 1. Account and Profile Information
- **Examples**: account name, email address, username, password hash, profile settings, contributor status
- **Retention period**: retained while the account is active and for up to 6 years after account closure, unless a longer period is required by law or legitimate business need
- **Purpose**: account administration, user authentication, service support, fraud prevention, legal compliance
- **Notes**: delete or anonymize once retention needs expire and no ongoing legal obligation exists

## 2. Contribution and Comment Content
- **Examples**: submitted stories, news tips, comments, editorial drafts, publication metadata
- **Retention period**: retained until removed by the user or as required for editorial or legal reasons; copies may remain for up to 6 years for dispute resolution or regulatory compliance
- **Purpose**: publication, editorial workflows, content moderation, dispute investigation
- **Notes**: published content may remain publicly accessible until removed under moderation policies; copies of removed content may be archived temporarily for moderation and legal review

## 3. Newsletter and Marketing Consent Records
- **Examples**: newsletter subscriptions, marketing opt-in/opt-out history, consent timestamps, consent categories
- **Retention period**: retained for the duration of the subscription and up to 6 years after unsubscribing or withdrawing consent, subject to legal obligations
- **Purpose**: consent management, direct marketing compliance, proof of lawful basis for processing
- **Notes**: maintain audit logs of consent changes to support POPIA accountability and GDPR record-keeping

## 4. Cookies, Analytics and Usage Logs
- **Examples**: session identifiers, cookie preferences, analytics events, page visits, device metadata, IP address hashes
- **Retention period**: retained in identifiable form only as long as necessary for functionality and security; aggregated or anonymized data may be retained indefinitely for analytics and reporting
- **Purpose**: website operation, performance optimization, security monitoring, user preference persistence
- **Notes**: analytics and marketing cookies are enabled only after consent; anonymize or delete raw logs consistent with stated consent and data minimization principles

## 5. Transactional and Payment Records
- **Examples**: invoices, subscription payment receipts, billing contacts, payment processor references, transaction dates
- **Retention period**: retained for up to 6 years or longer if required by tax, financial or other statutory obligations
- **Purpose**: accounting, audit, tax compliance, dispute resolution
- **Notes**: do not retain unnecessary payment card data; rely on payment processors for sensitive payment details wherever possible

## 6. Support Requests and Correspondence
- **Examples**: email support tickets, inquiry forms, complaint details, account recovery requests
- **Retention period**: retained for up to 6 years after resolution or closure, unless a shorter period applies under operational policy
- **Purpose**: customer support, incident investigation, legal defense, service improvement
- **Notes**: retain only information relevant to the support interaction and delete personal details when no longer needed

## 7. Security, Fraud and Abuse Records
- **Examples**: security incident logs, fraud investigations, blocked IP addresses, abuse reports, access control events
- **Retention period**: retained for up to 2 years or longer if needed to investigate, defend or comply with legal obligations
- **Purpose**: risk management, security assurance, regulatory reporting, legal defense
- **Notes**: maintain restricted access and review retention periodically to avoid excessive storage of personal security data

## 8. Legal and Compliance Records
- **Examples**: data subject request records, policy acknowledgment logs, processing activity records, vendor agreements, audit evidence
- **Retention period**: retained for at least 6 years or as required by regulatory or contractual obligations
- **Purpose**: demonstrate POPIA accountability, respond to regulator inquiries, support internal audit and compliance programs
- **Notes**: include retention of records of processing activities (ROPA) and data breach handling documentation

## 9. Third-Party Processor and Transfer Documentation
- **Examples**: processor contracts, standard contractual clauses, adequacy assessments, transfer impact reviews
- **Retention period**: retained for the duration of the processing relationship and for at least 6 years after termination
- **Purpose**: evidence of lawful cross-border transfers and processor oversight
- **Notes**: review agreements regularly and update retention obligations to reflect current service provider arrangements

## 10. Archiving, Backups and Deletion Controls
- **Examples**: backup snapshots, system archives, deleted data retention workflows, permanent deletion records
- **Retention period**: backups may retain personal data for a maximum of 90 days unless a longer retention period is justified by business continuity or legal requirements
- **Purpose**: disaster recovery, system restore, continuity planning
- **Notes**: ensure deleted data is purged from backup systems in accordance with policy and that restoration procedures incorporate data protection controls
